Distressed Assets

JLM's Distressed Credit & Opportunistic Investing Division was created to take advantage of volatility and cycles in the marketplace. During the last downturn, we acquired over $220 million worth of distressed commercial real estate, senior housing, and corporate aircraft assets. We look for opportunities that offer attractive risk-adjusted returns where we can capitalize on our ability to underwrite, bring capital, and close deals quickly.

Investment Criteria: Our focus is on loans and REO being sold on the secondary markets by banks, insurance companies, holdings companies, government agencies, private investors, and trustees. We buy loans and REO securitized by hard assets such as commercial and multifamily residential real estate, senior housing and corporate aircraft and equipment. We will consider individual assets, as well as portfolios.

Real Estate

JLM's Real Estate division and its principals acquire and manage both fee simple assets and loans in all areas of commercial and residential real estate. We currently have investments in Texas, Arizona, Colorado, Georgia, Illinois and Florida that include retail, industrial, medical, multifamily, self-storage, flex, senior housing and office buildings.

Acquisition Criteria: We acquire assets nationwide, with a preference in the South, Southeast, Midwest and Atlantic Coastal areas. Our priority assets are multifamily, retail, office, medical, senior housing, industrial, self-storage and flex.

Development Criteria: We have the background to do ground up development opportunities with a special area of interesting in multifamily, high rise luxury condominiums, and high rise luxury office buildings.

JLM Healthcare

JLM Healthcare Mission Statement: To create an environment for our business and shareholders that attracts and retains committed, innovative, caring personnel that serve the needs of each resident, family member, and employee. JLM Healthcare, with its concentration on senior housing, is a full service nursing home owner and operator with a focus on care of higher acuity patients. JLM currently owns 10 facilities in Illinois, which is comprised of 1,553 beds and is actively pursuing new investments in this sector.

Acquisition Criteria: JLM is looking for investment opportunities in SNF / ICF / CCRC that provide us the ability to acquire both the real estate and the operations. We prefer to make acquisitions in metro areas on a portfolio basis. Individual asset acquisitions are considered if they provide a strategic opportunity for future growth. JLM is dedicated to providing optimal care and an excellent employee environment; therefore we will only acquire assets with good reputations.

Development Opportunities: In addition to acquisitions, JLM purchases bed licenses for use in the construction and development of new skilled nursing facilities. Furthermore, we will consider direct purchases or leases with an option to buy of newly developed facilities.

Franchise Development

Larry Meyer in partnership with Trey Owen, through various entities have formed JLM Financial Partners to focus on the acquisition and development of Planet Fitness franchises . JLM Financial Partners is acquiring 8 existing Planet Fitness franchises located in Texas and Oklahoma as part of its initial investment.

Investment Criteria: As JLM Financial Partners can offer an existing Planet Fitness Franchisee a lot of flexibility in structuring a transaction to meet the individual needs of each franchisee. JLM Financial Partners can acquire existing Planet Fitness franchise club portfolios either with or without further development rights as well as single clubs if they are strategic in nature.
